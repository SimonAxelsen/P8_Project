Legend and interpretation guide

Use these notes to read trends, not absolute thresholds.

How to read the dashboard

Most signals are most reliable when you compare them over time. A sudden change is often more important than a single value. For clips, the highlighted window shows the current time focus.

Energy rises = louder or more active Pitch rises = higher f0Mean Boundary spikes = likely phrase breaks

Energy and activity

rmsDb

Signal energy in decibels. Higher values usually mean louder or stronger signal.

rms

Root-mean-square amplitude. Higher means more energy in the signal window.

noiseRms

Estimated background noise floor. If this rises, the environment is noisier.

snrLike

Approximate signal-to-noise ratio. Higher means the signal stands out more clearly.

vad

Voice activity state, usually 0 or 1. 1 means above the activity threshold.

pauseMs

Time since recent activity in milliseconds. Higher values indicate longer pauses.

speechMs

Duration of the current active region in milliseconds.

Pitch and voicing

f0Mean

Estimated fundamental frequency. Higher values indicate higher pitch.

f0Slope

Pitch trend over time. Positive values mean rising pitch, negative values mean falling pitch.

voicedRatio

How much of the current window appears voiced or periodic.

Spectral shape

zcr

Zero-crossing rate. Higher values often mean noisier or higher-frequency content.

specCentroid

Spectral brightness. Higher values mean brighter sound.

specRolloff

Frequency below which most spectral energy lies.

specFlatness

How noise-like the spectrum is. Higher values suggest flatter spectra.

specFlux

How quickly the spectrum changes.

lowEnergyRatio

Amount of energy in the low-frequency band.

speechEnergyRatio

Energy in the speech-focused band.

highEnergyRatio

Energy in the upper frequency band.

Confidence and prosody

speechConfidence

Heuristic score for how speech-like the current signal appears.

speechLike

Binary decision derived from speechConfidence.

boundaryConfidence

Likelihood of a phrase or segment boundary.

speechEnergySlope

Trend of speech-band energy. Negative means falling energy.

MFCC

mfcc0, mfcc1, mfcc2

Compact descriptors of spectral shape. Best for comparing regions.

mfccDelta0

Rate of change of the first MFCC term.

Segments

voiced: active and periodic content.

pause: low activity or inactive region.

boundary: likely transition point based on the current boundary heuristic.
